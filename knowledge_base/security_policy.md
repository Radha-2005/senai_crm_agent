# Security Incident Response Policy

## Overview
SenAI takes security threats seriously. This policy governs our response to security incidents, ransomware threats, and unauthorized access attempts.

## Threat Classification

### Category 1: Ransomware/Extortion Threats
**Definition**: Demands for payment to prevent data publication or restore access.

**Response Protocol**:
1. **DO NOT** engage with threat actors or acknowledge demands
2. Immediately escalate to Chief Security Officer (CSO)
3. Engage external incident response firm
4. Preserve all communications as legal evidence
5. Notify legal team within 1 hour
6. Consider law enforcement notification (Action Fraud UK / FBI)

**Communication Rule**: All external communications regarding active threats must be approved by Legal and CSO.

### Category 2: Data Breach
**Definition**: Unauthorized access to or exfiltration of customer data.

**Response Protocol**:
1. Isolate affected systems immediately
2. Preserve forensic evidence (do not wipe systems)
3. Notify DPO and Legal within 1 hour
4. Begin GDPR 72-hour notification countdown
5. Engage cybersecurity forensics team

### Category 3: Account Compromise
**Definition**: Unauthorized access to customer or internal accounts.

**Response Protocol**:
1. Immediately suspend compromised accounts
2. Force password reset for affected users
3. Review audit logs for data access
4. Notify affected customers within 24 hours

## Do NOT Do

- Never pay ransom demands
- Never delete evidence of an attack
- Never make public statements without Legal approval
- Never communicate exact scope of breach until forensics complete
